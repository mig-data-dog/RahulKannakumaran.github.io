Rahul's Ethical Reflection:


In recent years, Autonomous Robots have become increasingly relevant in many aspects of society such as e-commerce and medical fields, as well as transportation. This primarily due to the labor Autonomous Robots alleviates so that resources can be redistributed elsewhere. However, given the transparency of its thought process, Autonomous Robots raises ethical concerns.

This is especially relevant to the healthcare field, as doctors must take many things into factor before making an executive decision. Given its inability to explain the reason behind its decision, Autonomous Robots could be a liability to doctors, as doctors are unable to interpret and justify the robots’s reasoning and thought process to produce the result .

This also raises the question of who would be liable if a patient becomes misdiagnosed, as both the machine and human could be at fault. This argument would generally be taken to court however, since it may have been the machine’s fault, the blame could also be shifted to the company that implemented the Autonomous Robot system in the first place, which is why laws must be continuously iterated until foolproof before Autonomous Robots are widely implemented especially in health care.
